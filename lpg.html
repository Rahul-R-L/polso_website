LPG RETICULATION

LPG/CNG/PNG RETICULATION

LPG reticulation is a centralized gas distribution system that supplies Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG) to multiple users through a pipelined network. This system is widely used in residential apartments, commercial buildings, hotels, hospitals, and industrial setups, ensuring a safe, efficient, and continuous gas supply. Unlike traditional cylinder-based systems, LPG reticulation eliminates the need for individual gas cylinders, reducing handling risks, storage space, and supply interruptions. It enhances safety by incorporating leak detection systems, pressure regulators, and emergency shut-off valves, minimizing fire hazards. Moreover, it promotes cost-efficiency by optimizing gas usage, reducing wastage, and ensuring a steady supply without frequent refilling. As a sustainable and modern energy solution, LPG reticulation supports cleaner fuel adoption, making it an essential infrastructure for urban and industrial energy needs.

LOT LPG

What is LOT LPG?

LOT stands for Liquid Off-Take. It refers to a system where LPG is drawn in its liquid form from specially designed LOT cylinders, rather than in vapor form as in conventional cylinders. This liquid is then vaporized through an external LPG vaporizer before being supplied to the end users. LOT systems are commonly used in commercial and industrial applications where high gas flow rates and consistent pressure are needed, such as in hotels, hospitals, factories, or large kitchens. Compared to traditional vapor withdrawal cylinders, LOT systems are more efficient for high-consumption needs, reduce cylinder replacement frequency, and ensure a stable and reliable gas supply.

Advantages of LOT

High Gas Flow Rate

Capable of supplying large volumes of gas (upto 10kg/hr per cylinder), suitable for high-demand applications.

No Residual Loss

Ensures complete utilization of LPG from each cylinder compared to 100-500gm of LPG residue per VOT cylinder.

Space Efficiency

Requires less space compared to traditional bulk storage systems.

Liquid LPG is converted into vapour form using Vapourizer. Multiple safety devices are added in the gas system for improved safety.

LOT system includes Manifold, Vapourizer, Strainer, Shut-Off valves, Safety relief Valve, Pressure Regulators, etc

Improved Safety

Incorporates multiple safety features to minimize risks associated with LPG handling.

Consistent Pressure

Maintains stable gas pressure, enhancing operational efficiency.

Fewer Cylinder Replacements

Reduces the frequency of changing cylinders due to efficient liquid withdrawal.
